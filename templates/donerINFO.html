Am I eligible to donate blood?

Even though you may be enthusiastic about donating blood, you may not be eligible. This is determined through an initial screening process. The donor must be 18 to 65 years of age with a minimum weight of 45 kg. The blood collected needs to be clean and disease-free. Post-screening, all donated blood samples are tested to ensure that no diseases are transmitted.

The following people are not eligible for blood donation:

-Intoxicated individuals

-Intoxicated individuals

-Type 1 diabetes patients

-Patients having coronary heart disease or those who have suffered a heart attack

-APregnant and nursing women

-Patients with AIDS, Hepatitis B or C, Syphilis

-People who have a fever or other active infections

-People who have got a piercing or body art done during the last 6 months

Where to go and donate?

Associating with a trusted blood bank or hospital is extremely essential where all necessary procedures to collect blood in a safe manner are followed. Donation is done in a seated or lying position and is performed by a trained healthcare professional. A sterile needle is inserted in the elbow for 8-10 minutes for 350-450 ml of blood. Post this, the blood is collected in sample tubes.

Be well-hydrated and do not miss meals

One should have a proper meal and be adequately hydrated before donating blood to prevent weakness. Avoid fatty foods before donating as the fat can affect the blood processing tests. Eating iron-rich foods can help as it increases the haemoglobin.

Carry your prescription

Take note of prescribed medications, if any, and inform the staff during registration about the same. One may not realise but a simple medication taken for influenza can defer the blood donation process for two weeks.

Physical examination

The medical personnel on location notes down the donor’s medical history and conducts a small physical examination comprising of weight, pulse, blood pressure and haemoglobin level.

Arm bandage

Post blood donation, a small bandage is put on the area from where blood has been extracted. Keep this on for at least two-four hours. If you start to bleed, raise the arm above the head and apply pressure until it stops. If hurting persists, apply ice. If you’re not feeling well, contact the donation centre or your doctor.

Have a snack

Take adequate rest

Avoid intense exercise or heavy lifting for the day.

When can I donate again?

A man can donate blood every 90 days and women every 120 days; whereas platelets can be donated 24 times a year (approximately twice a month with a minimum gap of 48 hours in between).
